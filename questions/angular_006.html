What are directives in Angular?

Directives are special markers on a DOM element that tell Angular to do something with that element or its children. Angular has three types of directives: component directives, structural directives, and attribute directives.

Explanation:

- **Component Directives**: These are basically the components themselves.
- **Structural Directives**: These modify the DOM layout by adding or removing elements (e.g., `*ngIf`, `*ngFor`).
- **Attribute Directives**: These modify the appearance or behavior of an element (e.g., `[ngClass]`, `[ngStyle]`).
